Disasturvey is a small device that enables NGOs and volunteers to cheaply and quickly gather information from the local population to make good decisions and help where help is needed most.

How does it work?

The NGO staff records the questions to gather the data they will need to make decisions, such as how many people live in a household, if they have enough food, if somebody is injured and how serious. Allowing them to record a custom message and questions not only allows them to record in any language but also makes the device usable by many more people, in particular illiterate, people.
The NGO staff then puts the data on the devices and distributes them in a village, camp or general disaster region.
People listen to the questions, press the appropriate buttons and hand the device over to their neighbors.
The data is sent real-time to the staff’s computer which presents them with an analysis and visualises the data on a map to make decisions about their action plan.
